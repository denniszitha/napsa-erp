Risk Management Lifecycle

Risk Identification

Identify and document potential risks that could impact organizational objectives.

Key Activities:
  • Conduct risk workshops and brainstorming sessions
  • Review historical incidents and near-misses
  • Analyze industry trends and regulatory changes
  • Interview stakeholders and subject matter experts

Best Practices & Tips

Risk Identification

Use multiple sources: workshops, interviews, document reviews, and industry benchmarks. Don't rely on a single method.

Assessment Consistency

Use standardized scales and criteria across the organization. Provide clear definitions and examples for each rating level.

Documentation

Maintain comprehensive documentation including rationale for assessments, evidence, and decision justifications.

Regular Reviews

Schedule regular risk reviews based on risk levels. Don't wait for annual reviews to update critical risks.

Common Pitfalls to Avoid
  • Assessing risks in isolation without considering interdependencies
  • Focusing only on negative risks and ignoring opportunities
  • Setting and forgetting - risks need continuous monitoring
  • Over-relying on historical data without considering emerging risks
